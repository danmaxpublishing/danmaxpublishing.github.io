Performance Notes

Steady-state cost¶

  • Zero per-frame managed (GC) allocations while photo mode is open and idle and while adjusting sliders (after the first-open warmup frame, which builds caches). These are asserted by allocation gates in the automated test suite, not just measured once.
  • While closed, the controller only polls the toggle binding — no other per-frame work, no allocations.
  • Slider value labels use cached lookups and TextMesh Pro's non-allocating numeric SetText.

Capture wall-times¶

Measured in the demo scene (Unity 6000.3, mid-range desktop, 640×480 base render in the automated batch run). Informational, not a contract:

Supersample Wall time
1× ≈ 60–250 ms
2× ≈ 180–270 ms
4× ≈ 700–880 ms

Times scale roughly with output pixel count — a 4× capture of a 1080p view renders at 7680×4320 — dominated by the GPU readback and PNG encode; expect multiple seconds at 4× from 1080p. A PROCESSING… badge appears automatically during heavy supersampled captures (and never appears in the captured photo).

Memory¶

  • Repeated open/close cycles are leak-gated in the test suite: zero net scene-object growth and zero Unity-object growth over 100 cycles.
  • The UI prefab is instantiated on first open and reused afterwards.

Tips¶

  • Prefer 2× over 4× for social-media-sized output; 4× exists for print-quality shots.
  • Transparent captures render without post-processing (clean alpha), which also makes them slightly cheaper.
  • See Capture for the full capture pipeline.
